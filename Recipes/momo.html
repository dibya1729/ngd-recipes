Veg Momo

Description

A type of dumpling popular in Nepal and Tibet, veg momos are prepared by first making a dough from flour and water, kneading it until smooth and elastic. The dough is then rolled into thin circles. For the filling, finely chopped vegetables such as cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and onions are mixed with aromatics like garlic, ginger, and green onions, along with spices like cumin, coriander, and soy sauce. A spoonful of filling is placed in the center of each dough circle, which is then folded and sealed by pinching the edges together, creating a pleated pattern. The momos are steamed in a steamer basket until the dough becomes translucent and the filling is cooked through. They are typically served hot with a spicy dipping sauce, often made from tomatoes, chilies, and sesame seeds.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups finely chopped or grated mixed vegetables (e.g., cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, and onions)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped green onions (scallions)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oil (vegetable or sesame oil)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (optional)
  • For the dipping sauce (optional):
  • 2 tomatoes, blanched and peeled
  • 2-3 dried red chilies, soaked in warm water
  • 2-3 garlic cloves
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
